# Brief: "Day 4 of pretending I did not see her text"

**Format:** 5-slide carousel (expanded from 4 for pacing)
**Hook:** "POV: it has been 96 hours and the guilt is now load-bearing"
**Status:** Script Ready — pacing validated; the single-thread/new-timestamp gag is strong enough to script
**Visual tone:** Single chat thread, timestamps escalate, UI never changes — only the shame does

---

## Concept

One chat thread. One unanswered message. Five timestamps.
The joke is that nothing in the UI changes between slides — same message, same read receipt, same silence — but the timestamp keeps moving and the internal monologue gets worse.
The carousel is a meditation on the specific horror of the "2d" stamp becoming "4d."

## Creative Notes

- The single-thread-never-changes trick is the entire format. Do not add new bubbles, new characters, or reaction shots. The UI consistency is the gag.
- Timestamps must be legible on mobile — keep them top-right, bold, not gray-on-gray.
- Read-receipt checkmarks or "Read 9:14 AM" must be visible on every slide. They are the evidence.
- Mom's message should be warm and short. The joke breaks if she sounds demanding.
- No faces, no selfies, no "POV" acting. The phone is the entire set.

---

## Research Questions Before Scripting

- Does the "same screen, new timestamp" format hold attention for 5 slides, or does swipe-through drop at slide 3? A/B against a version that introduces a second chat on slide 4.
- Test: is `4d` funnier than `5d`? Both are universal but `4d` feels more specifically like a weekend-rolled-over moment.
- Could this become a recurring format? "Day 4 of..." templated carousels — pretending I didn't see the Venmo request, pretending I'm still going to the gym, etc.
