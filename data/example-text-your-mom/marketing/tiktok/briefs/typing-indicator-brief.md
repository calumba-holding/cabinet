# Brief: "Mom uses three dots and I lose a year of my life"

**Format:** 6-slide carousel
**Hook:** "She is typing. She has been typing. For nine minutes."
**Status:** Script Ready — priority bumped from Medium (most visually native concept in the backlog)
**Visual tone:** Extreme close-up on the typing indicator, stopwatch-style tension

---

## Concept

The typing indicator is the worst UI element ever invented.
It shows you that something is coming but not what it is. It strips you of agency and gives you nothing to do with the knowledge.
This carousel turns the three dots into the main character — a mini horror movie where the villain is punctuation that might become a paragraph.

---

## Slide Breakdown

### Slide 1 — The Sighting
**Visual:** Chat thread. Mom's side shows the typing bubble with three dots. Timestamp `9:02 PM`.
**Text overlay:** "She started typing at 8:53."
**Tone:** The dots are on. Everything else is still. We are already trapped.

### Slide 2 — The Catalog of Possibilities
**Visual:** Same typing indicator. Around it, floating ghost-text options: `"Can you call me"` / `"So"` / `"Who is this man in the photos"` / `"Dad and I have been talking"`.
**Text overlay:** "The dots contain every possible outcome."
**Tone:** Schrödinger's text. Every option is worse than the last.

### Slide 3 — The Pause
**Visual:** The three dots — but now ONLY TWO dots. (Looks broken. It's not. She stopped typing.)
**Text overlay:** "She stopped. Why did she stop."
**Tone:** The silence is the new threat. Typing was better.

### Slide 4 — The Resumption
**Visual:** Three dots again. Stronger contrast. The chat header reads `typing...` in bold.
**Text overlay:** "She started again. This is now a draft. A real draft."
**Tone:** Whatever she erased, what she's writing now is the edited version. That's worse.

### Slide 5 — The Delivery
**Visual:** Typing indicator disappears. New bubble lands. Content: `"ok"`
**Text overlay:** "Nine minutes. For 'ok'."
**Tone:** Punchline. Anticlimax as climax.

### Slide 6 — The Exit
**Visual:** App notification: `Text Your Mom — don't let her draft for 9 minutes. Reply before the dots start. 💬`
**Text overlay:** "The dots never start if you go first."
**Tone:** Clean. The product as pre-emptive relief.

## Creative Notes

- The typing indicator must be the EXACT iMessage/Android default. Do not stylize it. The familiarity is the joke.
- Slide 3 is the risk — "two dots instead of three" will only land if the difference is visible on mobile. Test by showing the slide to someone cold and asking what's different. If they don't notice in 2 seconds, reshoot with a more obvious visual.
- Ghost-text in Slide 2 should look like Apple Notes or a text preview, not a bubble. Light gray, italic, hovering.
- Slide 5's "ok" must be lowercase, no punctuation. That's what makes it funny.
- Zero trending audio. A ticking clock or phone-vibrate SFX is enough. Silence also works.

---

## Research Questions Before Scripting

- Does the typing-indicator gag translate to Android users, or is it iMessage-centric? Our audience skews iPhone but confirm.
- Is 6 slides too long for a single-visual premise? Test 5-slide version that cuts Slide 3 (the pause) — faster, but loses the horror beat.
- Could the "9 minutes" number be tested? "14 minutes" feels more absurd but less believable.
- The final "ok" could be a range: `"ok"`, `"k"`, `"👍"`. Which lands hardest?
